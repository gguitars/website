Path Jaguar

The Modern Offset

The Concept

Known for its versatility, my revision of the classic offset brings enhanced sharpness and precision to the format. We have stripped away the clunky vintage switching systems in favor of a streamlined workflow. [Image of electric guitar wiring diagram]

This marks my first bolt-on model, designed for players who need the snap and articulation that only a bolt-on neck can provide. It is a tool suitable for all genres, from shoegaze textures to precise math-rock.

Historical Context

"The Jaguar gathered dust until the punk explosion of the '70s took it from surf-rock relic to counter-culture icon. It defied initial expectations to become the standard-bearer for the alternative and indie rock sets."

Technical Specifications

Body Woods Alder, Swamp Ash, or Yellow Pine
Neck Construction Maple w/ Rosewood or Maple Fretboard
Electronics Giungi Single Coil, Humbucker, or P90

Path J Bass

The Low End Theory

The Concept

So innovative, yet so familiar. The Path J Bass anticipates your needs, offering a solid and stylish instrument that sounds as good as it looks. [Image of bass guitar anatomy]

We have refined the ergonomic contours to improve balance and comfort, allowing for extended playing sessions without shoulder fatigue. The extended 35" scale option provides tighter tension for drop tunings.

Historical Context

"Dedicated to the iconic Fender Jazz Bass. With its rich cultural legacy, championed by pioneers like Jaco Pastorius, this configuration blends output for a wide spectrum—from bright and crisp to warm and mellow."

Technical Specifications

Body Woods Okoume, Alder, Swamp Ash
Neck Specs 35" Scale, 12" Radius

Path SG

Sculpted Resonance

The Concept

Revitalized in design and fine-tuned to capture the sound of the 1960s in all its authenticity and gentle roughness.

Crucially, the Path SG design addresses the famous "neck dive" issue common in vintage models. Through careful weight distribution and body sculpting, we have created a balanced instrument perfect for exploring the upper register.

Historical Context

"A homage to a milestone of rock history. From Angus Young to Tony Iommi, the sharp double-cutaway shape has solidified its place as the weapon of choice for rock and metal."

Technical Specifications

Body Material Okoume or Mahogany
Fretboard Rosewood or Ebony (24.75" Scale)
Pickups Giungi Custom Humbucker or P90
Hardware Kluson Vintage Tuners, Gotoh Bridge

Path Les Paul

The Reason I Became A Luthier

The Concept

Simply eternal. The Path LP represents the perfect equilibrium between tone, sustain, and aesthetics.

My solid body construction minimizes unwanted feedback while maximizing sustain, providing distinct, rich tones—from the warm, mellow neck position to the powerful, biting bridge position.

Historical Context

"Introduced in the early 1950s, the combination of a solid mahogany body and a carved maple top created a sound that would define rock and roll history."

Technical Specifications

Body Base Old Growth Mahogany
Carved Top Flame Maple or Flame Ash
Neck Joint Deep Set-Neck Tenon
Electronics CTS Pots, Switchcraft, Paper-in-Oil Caps
