History_en: >
  With an iconic design and solid-body construction that minimises feedback
  and increases sustain, this guitar provides distinct, rich tones, allowing players
  to achieve a wide spectrum of sounds, from warm and mellow to powerful and biting.
  The Les Paul guitar was introduced in the early 1950s as the result of collaboration
  between Les Paul and Gibson. Its innovative features—including the solid body,
  humbucking pickups, and unmistakable design—contributed to its rapid success and
  enduring legacy in the world of music.
